# 01 · Directory Citation Submissions

**Goal**: 10 Australian business directories listed with consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on Day 1. Local citation count is one of Google's strongest local-pack ranking factors; consistent NAP across many sources is what builds it.

**NAP must match exactly across every submission** — Google compares strings character-for-character.

---

### Bonus / niche

- **Brisbane City Council** (if any local-area trader directory exists for the suburb)
- **Yeerongpilly Suburb Profile** sites (very low effort, very local)
- **Trades-specific apps** (Service Seeking, Airtasker — only if Jentech wants to use them for leads; otherwise skip)

---

## Suggested submission sequence (Day 1)

Work through 1–6 first. They're all free, accept submissions immediately, and don't require trade-association membership. Targets 7–8 follow. Items 9 and 10 are member-only — confirm Jentech's status first.

**Block out 90 minutes** to do all six. Each submission is roughly 10 minutes once the canonical NAP + description are open in another tab.

---

## After submission — track the citations

Create a simple tracking row per directory: date submitted, status (pending / live / rejected), live URL, and screenshot of the listing. Use this for the Month-3 trial review — citation count is one of the metrics that should visibly grow.
